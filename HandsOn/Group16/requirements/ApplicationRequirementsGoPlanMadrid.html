Application Requirements — GoPlan Madrid

1. Summary

The proposed application, GoPlan Madrid, integrates two open datasets — Municipal Cultural centers and Municipal cultural and leisure activities (in the next 100 days) — to support citizens and tourists in discovering new spots and alternative plans in the city and to promote participation in cultural activities (and the life of cultural centres). Users can explore by map, filtering by activity genre, location, or date, the upcoming activities available.

2. Scope

  • In: Visualization of municipal centres (location, type, service), upcoming municipal activities (next 100 days), interactive map and tabular exploration.
  • Out: ticket booking, activity updates by external users, integration of private/commercial datasets.

3. Actors & Use Cases

  • Actor: Citizen looking for plans in the city of Madrid.
  • UC1: Opening hours/schedule of specific cultural centres (the user can check if a museum is opened or closed at a specific time).
  • UC2: Check the activities of a specific quarter on specific dates (f.e. scheduled activities: Chueca AND October)
  • UC3: Compare city life between districts ( scheduled activities dd/mm/2025  in La Latina vs Chamberí).
  • UC4: Filter by activity genre (Theatre plays/ Sport Activities/ Arts..)

4. Functional Requirements (FR)

  • FR1: Visualize a map combining the municipal cultural center location and their corresponding upcoming cultural activities
  • FR2: Provide filters for events by category (e.g., music, theater, expositions…), date and hour range, and district
  • FR3: Compute a GoPlan Activity Index per district, based on the number of cultural centers and activities hosted in the 100 days
  • FR4: Make the map of municipal cultural centers interactive with markers showing their name, address, and type
  • FR5: Allow users to export selected data (centers, events, index) in CSC or JSON format

5. Non-Functional Requirements (NFR)

  • NFR1: Both municipal cultural centers and activities datasets, will be normalized into RDF using standards vocabularies such as:

-Schema.org/Place for centers

-WGS84 for coordinates

-DCAT for dataset metadata

  • NFR2: Code and data must be versioned in GitHub, with optional CI pipelines for RDF validation (using ShEx or SHACL).
  • NFR3: The open license (CC-BY 4.0) must be respected. Any reuse must cite the Ayuntamiento de Madrid as source and include the last update date
  • NFR4: The dataset will be accessible through a web interface or app, compliant with WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards, and responsive for desktop, tablet, and mobile.

6. Data Requirements

  • Municipal Cultural Centers dataset: center_id, name, address, district, coordinates (lat/lon), services
  • Municipal Cultural & Leisure Activities in the Next 100 Days dataset: event_id, title_activity, category, date, time, location, description, link (contentURL)
  • Linking strategy: spatial location - when only coordinates are provided, assign events to the nearest cultural center or district

8. Acceptance Criteria

  • FR1→ Visualize  map combining centers + activities

Given the two datasets, municipal cultural centers and activities in Madrid, when the map is loaded, the cultural and activities appear with distinct markers. Given a cultural marker, when clicked, then a popup shows the center’s name, address, and type, as well as its upcoming activities

  • FR2 → Filtering by category, date and district

Given the dataset of activities, when the user selects a category, then only activities of that category appear on the map and table. Same happens when the user filters by date range or by district, so the only activities that fall within that range or are in that district are shown.

  • FR3 → Compute GoPlan Activity Index

Given the datasets, when the system calculates the GoPlan Activity Index per district, then the result reflects the total number of cultural centers and activities scheduled in the next 100 days for that district. Given the map view, when a district is selected, then the Activity Index is displayed in the side panel.

  • FR4 – Interactive map of cultural centers

Given the dataset of cultural centers, when the map is loaded, then each center has a marker located at its coordinates. Given a user click on a center marker, then the popup shows its accessibility attributes and services in addition to address and type.

  • FR5 – Export data

Given a user selection of data , when the export button is pressed, then the data are downloadable in CSV format.Given a user selection of data, when the export format JSON is chosen, then the system provides a JSON file containing the same selection.
