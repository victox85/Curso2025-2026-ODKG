Madrid Transportation and Safety Data Analysis

Executive Summary

This document presents a comprehensive analysis of four datasets related to Madrid's transportation infrastructure and safety:

  • AccidentesBicicletas_2024: Bicycle accidents recorded in Madrid during 2024
  • Cabezas_Semaforo_Bici: Locations and details of bicycle traffic lights
  • PEATONES_2024: Pedestrian-related data for 2024
  • BICICLETAS_2024: Bicycle infrastructure and data

Analysis performed on: October 06, 2025

Overall Data Landscape

The datasets collectively provide a rich view of Madrid's urban mobility ecosystem. With over 350,000 total records spanning bicycle accidents, traffic infrastructure, and pedestrian patterns, this data enables comprehensive safety analysis and infrastructure planning.

The data quality is generally high, with most datasets showing complete or near-complete records across key fields. Geographic coordinates (latitude/longitude) are present across multiple datasets, enabling spatial analysis and integration with GIS systems.

Resource Naming Strategy

URI Pattern and Ontology Design

The following naming strategy has been defined for creating RDF resources and ontology URIs:

Base URIs

Base Ontology URI: http://madrid-transport-safety.org/ontology#
Base Resource URI: http://madrid-transport-safety.org/resource/

Naming Conventions

Resource Type Naming Pattern Example
Classes PascalCase singular nouns ont:Accident, ont:TrafficLight
Properties camelCase verbs/relations ont:hasLocation, ont:occurredOn, ont:involvesPedestrian
Individuals resource/{type}/{id} resource/accident/2024-001, resource/trafficlight/TL-12345
Data Properties camelCase descriptive ont:accidentDate, ont:latitude, ont:longitude

Domain-Specific Classes

  • ont:Accident - Represents bicycle accident events
  • ont:TrafficLight - Represents traffic light infrastructure
  • ont:PedestrianData - Represents pedestrian-related information
  • ont:BicycleStation - Represents bicycle infrastructure and stations
  • ont:Location - Represents geographical locations
  • ont:District - Represents Madrid districts
  • ont:Street - Represents street information

Key Object Properties

  • ont:hasLocation - Links entities to geographical locations
  • ont:hasDistrict - Links to administrative districts
  • ont:hasStreet - Indicates which street a location is on.
  • ont:involvesPedestrian - Indicates involvement of a pedestrian in an accident
  • ont:locatedInDistrict - Specifies the district where something is located
  • ont:nearTrafficLight - Spatial relationship indicating proximity to a traffic light.

Key Data Properties

  • ont:accidentDate - The date and time when an accident occurred
  • ont:latitude - The latitude coordinate of a location
  • ont:longitude - The longitude coordinate of a location
  • ont:deviceId - The unique identifier for the bicycle counting device
  • ont:streetName - The name of a street
  • ont:districtName - The name of a district
  • ont:districtCode - The official code of a district
  • ont:bicycleType - The type or category of the bicycle
  • ont:accidentType - Classification of the type of accident
  • ont:severity - The severity level of an accident
  • ont:weatherCondition - The weather conditions at the time of the accident
  • ont:timeOfDay - The time period of day when the accident occurred
  • ont:installationDate - The date when traffic light was installed
  • ont:identifier - A unique identifier for an entity
  • ont:address - The full address of a location
  • ont:bicycleCount - The number of bicycles counted at a station
  • ont:pedestrianCount - The number of pedestrian counted at a location
  • ont:pedestrianCrossingDate - The date and time of pedestrian crossing
  • ont:deviceType - The type or category of the counting device

Identifier Strategy

Unique identifiers are constructed using:

  • Dataset abbreviation (e.g., BA for Bicycle Accidents)
  • Year (e.g., 2024)
  • Sequential number or original ID
  • Example: resource/accident/BA-2024-00001

Vocabulary Reuse

Standard vocabularies integrated:

  • schema.org - For general entities (Place, Event)
  • GeoSPARQL - For geographical features
  • Dublin Core - For metadata (date, creator, source)
  • SKOS - For controlled vocabularies and classifications
